What to do if an avacado has anthracnose:

Orchard Sanitation: Remove and destroy any infected fruit, as well as fallen fruit and plant debris from the orchard. Proper disposal helps reduce the inoculum source and prevent further spread. Pruning and Canopy Management: Maintain good airflow and sunlight penetration within the avocado canopy by proper pruning and spacing of branches. This helps reduce humidity and create an environment less favorable for disease development. Irrigation Management: Avoid over-irrigation and excessive moisture around the avocado trees. Ensure proper drainage to minimize the availability of standing water that can promote disease spread. Fungicide Applications: Fungicides labeled for brown rot control can be applied during the fruit development stage. Consult with local agricultural extension services or experts for recommended fungicides and appropriate application timings. Follow the instructions on the fungicide label regarding rates, safety precautions, and pre-harvest intervals. Harvesting: Harvest avocados at the proper maturity stage and handle them carefully to minimize bruising or damage that can provide entry points for the pathogen. Resistant Varieties: Consider planting avocado varieties that have some level of resistance to brown rot. Resistant varieties can help reduce the severity of the disease.
